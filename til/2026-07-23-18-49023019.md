### Today I Learned (TIL)

TIL that relying solely on software factories, or "harness engineering," is not sufficient to ensure the long-term success of a complex software system. According to the article "Why Software Factories Fail (or: harness engineering is not enough)", a software factory's effectiveness can be undermined by factors such as inadequate contextual understanding, poor communication among stakeholders, and an over-reliance on automation without human judgment. I'll take this as a reminder to prioritize "advanced context engineering" and consider the human aspects of software development, including the importance of domain knowledge, collaboration, and adaptability, when building and maintaining large-scale systems.
