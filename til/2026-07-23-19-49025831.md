### TIL: Solid-State Separation of Rare Earth Elements

TIL that researchers at the University of Chicago have developed a novel solid-state "atomic channel" for separating rare earth elements. This breakthrough has potential implications for the efficiency and sustainability of electronics manufacturing, where rare earth elements are a critical component in the production of magnets, energy storage devices, and other high-tech components. As a developer working on embedded systems, I see this as a significant advancement that could enable more efficient recycling and reuse of these elements, potentially reducing electronic waste and the environmental impact of our industry.
