**TIL: Foldkit's Approach to Frontend Frameworks**

I came across Foldkit, a frontend framework inspired by Elm's architecture and built on top of the Effect library. This caught my attention as it proposes a unique approach to building correct frontend applications. The key takeaway is Foldkit's emphasis on functional reactive programming (FRP), which aims to eliminate the need for manual error handling and state management. By using a declarative programming paradigm, Foldkit's architecture encourages developers to reason about their application's behavior at a higher level of abstraction, potentially leading to more robust and maintainable codebases. I'm intrigued by the potential implications of this design, particularly in the context of large-scale frontend applications, and plan to explore Foldkit further to better understand its strengths and weaknesses.
